Experiment 2 - Living Impressions

Description

I picked a photo I took that day at the Santa Cruz cliffs for my impressionistic creation. I started with a very simple “horizontal division”: HORIZON_Y divides the canvas into two halves, sky and sea, and then superimpose layer by layer. I randomly placed some clouds in the sky to make the picture less monotonous, and I made a cliff layer based on Berlin noise, which I got inspired by the level of CMPM170. On the sea I made 6 sine + noise mixed wavebands, which, like the clouds, will scroll over time with zOff, and then there are random birds. The reimagine button can replace all seeds, and all the above noises are resampled.

Technical

The only global time variable, zOff, is incremented by 0.00055/frame and is shared by the clouds and waves. For the clouds I chose to store 3 random base values (x, y, size) for each cloud; a smooth drift is achieved in draw() using noise(base, zOff). I wrote some more about the use of noise in the Description. I didn't use any bitmap textures, I couldn't really understand what that was about. I only used the built-in primitives of p5.

Reflection
